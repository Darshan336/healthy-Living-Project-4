Butternut Squash soup

  1. Step 1: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Step 2: Cut the butternut squash in half lengthwise. Scoop out the seeds and place the halves on a baking sheet, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and roast for about 45-50 minutes or until the squash is tender.
  3. Step 3: While the squash is roasting, heat a large pot over medium heat. Add a bit of olive oil and sauté the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and apple until softened, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Step 4: Once the squash is done roasting, let it cool slightly, then scoop out the flesh and add it to the pot with the sautéed ingredients.
  5. Step 5: Pour in the vegetable broth and add the cinnamon and nutmeg. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes.
  6. Step 6: Use an immersion blender or transfer the soup in batches to a regular blender to puree until smooth. If using a regular blender, be sure to let the soup cool slightly and blend in batches.
  7. Step 7: Return the soup to the pot and reheat if necessary. Taste and adjust seasonings with salt and pepper.
  8. Step 8: Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

Ingredients

  • Ingredient 1: 1 medium-sized butternut squash
  • Ingredient 2: 1 medium onion, chopped
  • Ingredient 3: 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Ingredient 4: 1 carrot, chopped
  • Ingredient 5: 1 apple, peeled, cored, and chopped
  • Ingredient 6: 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Ingredient 7: 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Ingredient 8: 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Ingredient 9: Salt and pepper to taste
  • Ingredient 10: Olive oil for cooking

Macros

  • Protein: 2 grams
  • Fat: 3 grams
  • Carbohydrates: 25 grams
